01 / ORIGINS

1950 — 1975

POST-WAR ARCHITECTURE

New Brutalism emerged in post-war Britain as a reaction against decorative architecture. Architects like Alison and Peter Smithson championed "honesty" in materials—exposing structural elements rather than hiding them behind facades.

ARCHITECTURE MODERNISM

BÉTON BRUT

The term "brutalism" derives from the French "béton brut" (raw concrete). Le Corbusier's Unité d'Habitation exemplified the aesthetic: monumental scale, modular repetition, and unfinished concrete surfaces that revealed formwork patterns.

MATERIAL CONCRETE

SOCIAL HOUSING

Brutalist principles were widely adopted for social housing projects across Europe. The style promised functional, affordable housing at scale, though many projects later faced criticism for maintenance issues and inhospitable environments.

03 / DIGITAL REVIVAL

2010 — PRESENT

NEO-BRUTALISM ON THE WEB

In the 2010s, web designers began translating brutalist principles to digital interfaces. The movement rejected the flat, minimal aesthetics dominating the era (iOS 7, Material Design) in favor of raw, structural honesty.

Key characteristics include: default system fonts, visible borders and grid structures, high contrast color schemes (often black/white with a single accent), minimal animation, and deliberately exposed UI chrome like scrollbars and form elements.

Sites like Brutalist Websites (brutalistwebsites.com) catalogued hundreds of examples, creating a community around the aesthetic. The style found particular favor among artists, musicians, experimental agencies, and designers seeking to differentiate their portfolios.

CHARACTERISTICS
  • System fonts only
  • Visible borders
  • High contrast palettes
  • Exposed UI elements
  • Asymmetric layouts
  • Minimal shadows
  • Large typography
  • Raw, unpolished feel

04 / CASE STUDIES

BLOOMBERG.COM (2017)

EDITORIAL

Bloomberg's redesign embraced brutalist typography with condensed headlines, tight grids, and monochromatic palette punctuated by financial data visualizations.

BALENCIAGA.COM (2019)

FASHION

Luxury fashion's embrace of brutalism: stark product photography, system fonts, and a deliberately anti-commercial presentation that felt exclusive through rawness.

ARTIST PORTFOLIOS

CREATIVE

Countless designers and artists adopted neo-brutalism for portfolios, using the style to communicate confidence, technical skill, and a rejection of trends.
